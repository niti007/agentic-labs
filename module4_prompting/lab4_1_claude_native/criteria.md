# Urgency criteria — exactly what each label means

These are the rules for sorting incoming customer feedback by urgency. The whole point
is to be **specific**, so the AI (and any teammate) stops guessing what "important" means.

## CRITICAL  → handle within 1 hour
A message counts as CRITICAL if **any** of these is true:
- The customer says they will **cancel, leave, or stop paying**.
- They mention **legal action, a lawyer, a regulator, the press, or a public post**.
- **Money was lost or wrongly charged** (a billing error, a double charge, a failed refund).
- A **safety, security, or privacy** problem (data leak, account hacked, personal info exposed).

## MAJOR  → handle the same day
- A core feature is **broken or blocking their work**, but they are **not** threatening to leave.
- Strong frustration or repeated problem, with **no** cancellation/legal/money/safety trigger above.

## MINOR  → handle this week
- A small annoyance, a question, a feature suggestion, or **praise**.
- Nothing is blocked and nobody is upset enough to leave.

## When unsure
If a message could be CRITICAL **or** MAJOR, treat it as the **higher** one (CRITICAL).
It's safer to over-prioritize a real problem than to miss it.
